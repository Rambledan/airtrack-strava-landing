Why Pro, if you care about performance

The air you train in shapes the results you get.

You optimise sleep, fuel and mileage. Air is the input almost nobody measures — yet the research links even small changes in exposure to real differences in how you perform, recover and feel. Pro turns that into signals you can act on.

🏁
~16s slower over a marathon per +10% above the WHO limit

Race potential

Studies of marathon fields link higher particulate exposure to measurably slower finish times (around 16s for men, 12s for women per +10% above the WHO PM2.5 guideline). Pro flags the cleaner windows so race and key sessions land on your best air.

📈
Cleaner air = more from the same effort

Training effectiveness

When you train hard you breathe harder — pulling more of whatever's around you deep into your lungs. Pro tracks the exposure behind each session so you can see whether a flat day was your legs, or your air, and schedule quality work accordingly.

🧠
Emerging cognitive links

Brain & focus

A growing body of research associates higher long-term air pollution exposure with reduced cognitive performance and sharper focus costs. The evidence is still developing, so Pro shows your exposure trend rather than a false precise number — awareness you can factor in.

🌱
+11% new mental-health risk in more-polluted areas

Mood & wellbeing

People who moved to a more particle-polluted area showed around an 11% higher risk of a new mental-health problem than other movers. Pro helps you keep your cumulative exposure — and its knock-on effect on how you feel — trending the right way.

🫁
Respiratory risk rises with exposure — and falls when it drops

Respiratory health

Higher particulate and NO₂ exposure is linked to increased asthma and COPD risk — but it cuts both ways: one Clean Air Zone cut GP visits for respiratory illness by 25% in a year. Pro's cleaner-route and timing tools help you take the lower-exposure option by default.

🧭
24/7 — not just when you press record

Your full daily dose

Races are won on the other 23 hours too. Pro tracks exposure across your commute, work and home — automatically — so the picture reflects everything you breathe, not only your logged sessions.

A note on the science: these figures come from peer-reviewed research and are straight-line estimates — a compass, not a clinical forecast. Getting started & getting the most from it

From this page to air-in-your-feed in about 2 minutes

Here's exactly what happens after you tap download — and how to make sure your air quality shows up on every activity.

1

Download AirTrack

Tap the button to open the App Store, then install AirTrack. It's free and works on iPhone and Android.

2

Create your free account

Open the app and sign up with email or Apple/Google. Takes under a minute — no card needed for Free.

3

Connect Strava

Go to Settings → Connect Strava, sign in, and approve the permissions (see below). This is what lets AirTrack write air quality into your activities.

4

Record an activity

Head out and record a run, ride or walk in Strava as normal, then save it. No need to open AirTrack while you move.

5

See your air in the feed

Within a few minutes of saving, AirTrack adds your air quality to the activity description — visible to you and your followers.

6

Want it on every activity?

Free covers every 3rd activity. Upgrade to Plus (99p) for unlimited, or Pro for 24/7 tracking and performance insights — all in the app.

🔒 What the Strava permissions mean

  • •View your public profile — required to connect AirTrack to your Strava account.
  • •View your activities — so AirTrack knows where and when you moved, to calculate your air quality.
  • •View private activities — optional. Tick it if you want air quality on private activities too; untick to keep those out.
  • •Upload to Strava — lets AirTrack add the air-quality line to your activity description. You can add your own text too; we simply append to it.

You're always in control — disconnect anytime in the app or in your Strava settings. AirTrack never posts anything except the air quality line you asked for.

Quick answers

Before you download

Do I need new hardware or a wearable?

No. AirTrack uses your phone's location and high-resolution air quality data — there's nothing to buy or wear. It works alongside the Strava you already use.

Do I have to use Strava?

No, but it's the easiest way. Free and Plus add air quality to your Strava activities. If you'd rather not use Strava, you can log activities in the app directly, and Pro tracks your day automatically in the background.

What's the difference between Free, Plus and Pro?

Free adds air quality to every 3rd activity. Plus (99p/month) adds it to every activity, unlimited. Pro (£6.99/month) adds 24/7 exposure tracking without recording everything, plus performance, cleaner-route and wellbeing insights.

Will it drain my battery?

Free and Plus rely on the activities you already record in Strava, so there's no extra background load. Pro's 24/7 tracking uses efficient background location — you can tune accuracy in settings to balance battery and precision.

Is my data private?

Yes. AirTrack is GDPR-compliant, never shares your personal data without consent, and lets you disconnect or delete your account anytime. Air quality is the only thing written to your Strava feed.
